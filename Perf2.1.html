Each analyst tracks a universe of companies that they monitor regularly – investment opportunities may come from this existing coverage universe or ad-hoc through the team’s network. Analysts prepare preliminary financial analysis and present findings to the Portfolio Management Group, who decide if the team should proceed with due diligence. Once approved, the due diligence process begins and may include detailed financial analysis, meetings with industry experts / target companies’ management teams, product testing, peer analysis and assessment of sell-side opinions. Highly iterative process with analysts may take a month or longer. Due diligence findings are assembled into a formal write-up for the Portfolio Management Group.

Once an investment is approved, positions are sized from a top-down perspective, according to its risk-reward relative to other investments in the portfolio.
